Digital Memory Project

Exploring Digital Narratives and Memory in the Modern Age

Project Overview

The Digital Memory Project is an innovative research initiative dedicated to understanding how digital platforms shape our memories.

Research Goals

  • Investigate the role of digital technologies in memory preservation and dissemination
  • Analyze how individuals curate and present their digital identities
  • Explore the intersection of collective memory and social media narratives
  • Exploring reasons of why people post personal memories online

Project Scope

Our research encompasses multiple dimensions of digital memory, including personal memories online and offline spaces. We employ both qualitative and quantitative methodologies to provide comprehensive insights into how digital spaces function as memory vessels in the 21st century.

Research Team

Dr. Divya Bhatia

Principal Investigator

Dr. Divya Bhatia is an associate professor at Jindal School of Psychology and Counselling (JSPC), O.P. Jindal Global University. During her PhD at Sapienza University, Rome, Italy her work focused on the cognitive processes through which self and other’s performed actions come to influence one’s memory. At JSPC and JGU, she extends her research to understanding cognition in the digital world, and focuses on questions such as how do interactive digital contexts (e.g., social media, multitasking platforms, AI interfaces) alter the way we encode, remember, process information and relate to others? As Digital Memory Research Team, we are focused on understanding how human cognition, particularly memory, is being reconfigured in the digital age by the use of the Internet, social media and AI tools.

Dr. Arpita Gupta

Co-Principal Investigator

Dr. Arpita Gupta is an Assistant Professor at the Jindal School of Psychology and Counselling and a registered Clinical Psychologist with extensive academic, research, and clinical experience. Trained at institutions including IIT Kanpur, the Central Institute of Psychiatry (Ranchi), and the University of Delhi, her work sits at the intersection of mental health, identity, society, and critical qualitative research. A recipient of the David B. Feinsilver Award and multiple national fellowships and grants, she actively contributes to global and Indian conversations on mental health through research, teaching, clinical practice, and community outreach.

Dr. Kirti Tyagi

Co-Principal Investigator

Dr. Kirti Tyagi is an Assistant Professor at the Jindal School of Psychology and Counselling, where she also serves as Program Director of the School Grant Committee. She completed her PhD in the Department of Humanities and Social Sciences at the Indian Institute of Technology Delhi. Her doctoral research examined personality, contextual, and cognitive factors underlying positive and negative risk-taking across early, middle, and late adolescence. She has developed a domain-specific scale and a theoretical framework for positive risk-taking in adolescents. Her research interests include adolescent risk-taking and decision-making, psychometrics, digital games for social-emotional learning, cognition and emotion, resilience, and well-being.
