Current Study Opportunities

Study 1: Understanding personal memories in and out of social media

OPEN

The characteristics and content reliability of personal memories

Study 1 is focused on understanding whether the personal memories that are shared on social media differ in importance, novelty and emotionality from the memories not shared on social media during offline recall. Moreover, we are also interested to understand whether the recall of personal memories shared on social media differs in content reliability (accuracy and consistency) compared to those not shared on social media.

Purpose of posting personal memories online, social media engagement, and personality

Study 1 is also focused on understanding the relationship between the motives of posting personal memories online, use of social media, and personality of people posting personal memories in real life.

Study 2: Validating the 20-item Purposes of Online Memory Sharing Scale (POMSS) in the Indian context

OPEN

Confirm the four-factor structure of POMSS

Confirm the four-factor structure of POMSS in an Indian sample, addressing cultural relevance and psychometric robustness.

Establish the criterion validity of POMSS

Establish the criterion validity of POMSS by correlating its subscales with real-world social media posting behaviors, using a qualitative approach (e.g., content analysis) to classify behavioral patterns into the four motivational categories described by POMSS.

Examine age and gender differences

Examine age and gender differences in the four dimensions of POMSS, providing culturally informed insights into the motivations for online memory sharing in the Indian context.
